Stereoscopy

"The mind perceives an object of three dimensions by means of the two dissimilar pictures projected by it on the two retinae."

- Charles Wheatstone, 1838

This interactive animation illustrates how two cameras, or retinae, work together to create a stereograph image.

Toggle the left and right cameras (A and B) to see how it affects the 3D image of the cube. The top-down representation of it on the right illustrates each cameras line-of-sight towards the cube, showing you how much of each corner of the cube is visible to each camera at its current position.
